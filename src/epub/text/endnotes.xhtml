“Susemihl” refers to Franciscus Susemihl [Aristotelis Ethica Eudemia] Eudemi Rhodii Ethica (Teubner: 1884), who has brought much light by obvious corrections and judicious punctuation. Where readings other than his are adopted they are mentioned with the names of their authors. W. D. Ross’s suggestions have been of the greatest use to me; Adolf Theodor Hermann Fritzsche’s commentary (Aristotelis Ethica Eudemia Manz: 1851) I have sometimes referred to with advantage, and also to some notes printed by Prof. Henry Jackson and kindly sent me by him some years ago. Prof. Jackson is also the author of an article in the Journal of Philology, xxxii, which has shed a flood of light on the corrupt passage, book VII, chapters 13 and 14. Of course the principal help to the understanding of the two treatises is the Nicomachean Ethics, whose resemblances to and differences from this work are of great interest.
